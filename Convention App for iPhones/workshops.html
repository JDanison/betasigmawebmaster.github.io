Growing Strong: Tips, Tricks, and Strategies for your Best Recruitment Yet!

Hosted by Katie Langley

A strong recruitment forms the foundation for strong chapters - and it's not just the VPM's responsibility. It is important for chapters to work together and form a strategic approach to identifying potential new members, encouraging membership, and making "the sell." By focusing on broad recruitment strategies, conversation techniques, and how to avoid common pitfalls, this workshop will give you the tools to ensure your best recruitment yet! Remember when you play the Game of Recruitment, you play to win!

This workshop will be held in Salon C from 4:00 - 5:00 p.m. on Saturday.

Your Chapter Sponsor: Tapping the Untapped Resource

Hosted by Derrick Mills

How often do you talk to your sponsor? Do know who is your chapter sponsor (don’t answer that)? Every chapter is different and unique, but all must have a chapter sponsor. It can be difficult to find ways to get your chapter sponsor or Director of Bands interested in your chapter’s activities. A chapter sponsor can be more than an individual just approving paperwork. Derrick Mills, (TBSigma Chi Chapter Sponsor) will discuss strategies and ideas on how your chapter can improve sponsor/chapter relations and create a great partnership.

This workshop will be held in Salon E from 4:00 - 5:00 p.m. on Saturday.

Personal Accountability

Hosted by Marc Renaud

This workshop will take a look at tools we can use to hold ourselves and each other accountable as brothers of Kappa Kappa Psi. For this discussion will we draw upon sections of the ritual that can be used to reaffirm who we are as brothers and what we strive to accomplish. "We Love You, You're Perfect...Now Change": Recruiting and Embracing a Diverse Membership

Hosted by Erika Pope

It is an honor to be selected to serve. However, the many ways in which we serve our bands, though drawn from a common place of commitment, has proven to be very unique and diverse from campus to campus. Band service doesn't just happen, it takes an elite group of individuals pulling together to achieve the highest level of functionality; so how do we maintain a legacy of organizational dependability and ensure that we have "the right people in the right seats" on the bus of band service? In this workshop we'll be discussing the reasons we recruit and why considering diversity in recruitment strategies is important. We'll also discuss ways to embrace the differences between our members for the enhancement of the chapter experience. Like a lot of things in life: It's all about relationships!

This workshop will be held in Salon A from 9:30 - 10:30 a.m. on Saturday.

Joint Relations: Finding the Right Chemistry

Hosted by Jack Lee

Kappa Kappa Psi and Tau Beta Sigma – two things that go together like cookies and milk. But what happens when the interaction is more like oil and water? Do you find common ground and do what is best for your band program and the moment, or do you come to a standstill with no idea of what to do next? This workshop is built to help Sisters and Brothers understand each other, find common ground, and develop the best ways to work together, while also giving the other organization the space that we all sometimes need.

“Old Wine in New Bottles”: Embracing New Ideas in Traditional Chapter Functions

Hosted by Nicholas Bratcher

Arguably, one thing any successful chapter must do is have consistent membership intake.
